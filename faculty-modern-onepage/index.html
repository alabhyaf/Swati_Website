Swati Mandloi

I am a student in the field of environment aspiring to deliver methods of conscientious management of resources and valuation of the environment around us. I offer tested skills in economic analysis, environmental impact assessment, and project management. I have dedicated my life to the cause of sustainability and aspire to understand the impacts of government policies and commercial activities on environmental systems around us. I believe this can be done by mindful policy-making considering the economic value of the environment and consumer behaviour.

  • Scientific Monitoring Program: Pulau Hantu

    August 2017- October 2017

    Course Work

    The project required to proposed a scientific monitoring program that would be suitable for preserving the natural conditions of Pulau Hantu Island in Singapore keeping in mind the surrounding industrial activities. The monitoring program involved establishing baseline conditions and identifying parameters that are important and should be monitored to determine possible impacts from present activities. There included sedimentation rates, water quality monitoring, and biodiversity assessment (benthic and fish populations). The project also covered spatial distribution of monitoring sites along with temporal frequency for optimizing management.

  • Environment Law: Basel Convention

    August 2017- October 2017

    Course Work

    This law assignment required to write an argument to the government of Bangladesh on the importance of environmental governance and the Basel ban amendment. This paper focused on control of transboundary movement of hazardous substances regarding the ship-breaking industry of Bangladesh. Tackling the economic impact of the industry along with issues of worker and environment safety, the paper provided a legal point of view on the ramifications of ratification and ban amendment and improving internal governance. It also included some shortcomings of the Basel convention itself and recommended proposal to address these to respective bodies for better protection of economic activities and the environment in Bangladesh.

Research

Research Summary

Recent trends in deep-submicron very large-scale integration (VLSI) circuit technology have resulted in new requirements for algorithms in integrated circuit layout. Much of my work centers on new formulations that capture performance and density criteria in the physical layout phases of computer-aided design (CAD). Our results include near-optimal approximation algorithms for such computationally difficult problems as minimum-cost Steiner tree routing, low-skew clock networks, cost-radius tradeoffs, bounded-density trees, circuit probe testing, high-performing Elmore-based constructions, layout density control, and improved manufacturability.
